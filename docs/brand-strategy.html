Brand Strategy

How to present Petrica Dulgheru to the world — naming, architecture, logo, and positioning.

1. The Core Problem

You have three entities (REDI, BelgaLabs, personal), three domains of expertise (inclusive finance, chemistry, venture building), and four audiences (investors, corporates, governments, fellow entrepreneurs). Most branding advice would say "pick one." But your unique value IS the integration. So the brand needs to hold all three worlds together without feeling scattered.

2. Brand Architecture: Three Options

Option A

Personal Name Brand

petricadulgheru.com

You ARE the brand. Everything flows through your name. REDI and BelgaLabs are listed as "ventures" underneath.

+ Maximum credibility. People invest in people. Your story IS the differentiator. Scales with you to boards, speaking, books.

- Harder to spell/remember for non-Romanian audiences. Doesn't signal what you do.

Option B

Concept Brand + Personal

threeworlds.eu or 3worlds.co

The "Three Worlds" concept becomes the umbrella brand and the visual identity (planets). Your name sits inside it: "Petrica Dulgheru | Three Worlds."

+ Memorable. Tells your story instantly. The planet visual writes itself. Works across all audiences. Domain likely available.

- Requires explaining what "Three Worlds" means (but the website does this naturally).

Option C

BelgaLabs as Main Brand

belgalabs.com

Use BelgaLabs as the commercial entity and personal brand wrapper. You are the face of BelgaLabs.

+ Already exists. Feels like a company (good for B2B). "Labs" implies innovation.

- Doesn't convey the social mission or your personal story. Sounds like a tech startup, which undersells the NGO/policy/impact side. Limits you to the consulting identity.

Option D

Hybrid: Personal + Three Worlds Sub-brand

petricad.com with "Three Worlds" as tagline

Short, clean personal domain. "Three Worlds" appears in the tagline and visual system but isn't the brand name.

+ Personal. Short domain. "PD" initials work as a logo mark. Three Worlds stays as a visual concept.

- "PetricaD" is informal. May feel incomplete to institutional audiences.

3. My Recommendation

Go with Option B: "Three Worlds" as the umbrella concept, your name as the authority

Here's why this works for ALL your audiences simultaneously:

For investors: "Three Worlds" frames you as someone who sees across silos — exactly what impact investors want.
For corporates: It signals range and depth — you bring science, finance, and strategy.
For governments/EU: It positions you as a cross-disciplinary thinker, not a single-issue advocate.
For entrepreneurs: It says "I've built in multiple domains and I can help you too."

The tagline: "Science. Capital. Community." — three words, three worlds, instantly clear.

4. Logo Concepts

Three directions, all built on the "three worlds" visual language we already established.

Concept A — Three Orbits

The Orbital Mark

Three circles of different sizes (gold, blue, green) arranged in an orbital system. Simple, iconic, works at any size. The main mark for the brand.

Concept B — The Monogram

PD + Three Dots

Your initials "PD" with three colored dots (gold, blue, green) forming an orbit around the letters. Clean for business cards, email signatures, app icons.

Concept C — Minimal Mark

Three Spheres Aligned

The absolute simplest version. Three circles in a row — descending or ascending in size. Like a solar system viewed from the side. Works as a favicon, watermark, social avatar.
