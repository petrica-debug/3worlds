About

A scientist who
builds institutions

Born in a Roma village in Romania. Educated through the sacrifices of an older brother who worked night shifts so I could stay in school. I got a PhD in chemistry, moved to Belgium, and built a career in catalysis research — ruthenium catalysts, ammonia-to-hydrogen cracking, automotive emissions.

Then I went back. Friends I'd played football with couldn't find work. They didn't ask for money — they asked for jobs. I started businesses to employ them. Then I asked: how do I scale this beyond the people who know me?

That became REDI — the Roma Entrepreneurship Development Initiative — and the REDI Fund, the world's first investment vehicle for Roma entrepreneurs. I did an MBA to sharpen the strategy. Started the KU Leuven AI master — then chose to spend that time with my three daughters and build a company instead of writing another thesis. I already had a PhD; I didn't need to prove I could write. I needed to prove I could ship.
